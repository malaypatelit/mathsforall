Grade 1 Science Concepts

1. Life Systems: Living Things

  • Needs and Characteristics: Understanding that plants and animals require food, water, air, and shelter to survive.
  • The Five Senses: Exploring how sight, smell, touch, taste, and hearing help humans interact with the world.
  • Physical Features: Comparing the parts of different living things (e.g., stems vs. legs).

2. Earth and Space Systems: Daily and Seasonal Changes

  • The Four Seasons: Identifying cycles in weather and how they affect clothing, plants, and animal behavior (like hibernation).
  • Day and Night: Observing the Sun’s position and the predictable rhythm of the 24-hour cycle.
  • Environmental Stewardship: Basic conservation practices like recycling and protecting local habitats.

3. Matter and Energy: Properties and Sources

  • Materials: Sorting objects by texture, color, and buoyancy (sinking vs. floating).
  • Energy: Identifying the Sun as a primary source of heat and light.
  • Forces: Introduction to how objects move through pushing and pulling.

4. Scientific Inquiry & STEM

  • Observation: Using simple tools (magnifying glasses, rulers) to record data.
  • Prediction: Making "educated guesses" about what will happen during an experiment.
  • Design: Building simple structures to solve problems.
